I just realized how little I know about the IB program.

 

IBA: IB Americas

 

Last year 1.1% of all IB students got 45 points.

(However 70% of students pass, with average of 29 points)

 

To obtain a diploma, you must get at least 24 points. To pretty much guarantee that you will get a diploma, score at least 30.

 

The most popular Extended Essays are on History, however English and Biology is fairly high.

 

How to study for IB? (i took these from other people)

Spend your hours as such: 45 minutes of study, then 15 minutes of break. Make sure that when you study, you have absolutely nothing to distract you.

Study routines can vary, as certain subjects need to be prioritized at different times.

 

Your fundamental understanding is very important. Focus on learning the techniques you may need. There may be some techniques/tricks/solutions that your textbooks don't cover, and you can learn these from doing past papers.

 

As always, make sure you're focused when you're studying. Remove any sources of distractions like your phone, etc.

 

Work with friends. You may not always have the best, or even right approach to a question - and they might. Sometimes it's the other way around. However, if you can explain a question to a friend and if they can understand it, then it means you know the topic well.

It is very difficult to cram maths and I would strongly advise against it. Start revising early so you're in good shape for the exam.

 

 

 

 

What IB classes do I take?

 

If you didn't know:

HL classes: 2 years. hard

Sl classes: 1 year, not as hard

IB tests are scored out of 7 points, you take them in November or May.

The IB Diploma is scored out of 45 points. In order to obtain this score you will have to have had a perfect score in all of the IB classes you took. In order to get a Diploma, you must need 24 points; however, to guarantee that you can get one, you should aim for 30 points. A reasonable goal if you are ambitious is about 38 points.

TL; DR

Group 1: Do not take any HLs unless you like English

Group 2: Do not take any IB class from this subject

Group 3: You can take Economics, which has really good pass rates. Do NOT take history.

Group 4: I would recommend to take at least Biology from this group to have something to write an Extended Essay on. Taking another science if you like it will never hurt.

Group 5: Take math HL if you like math, and take math SL if you don't like it.

Group 6: meh
